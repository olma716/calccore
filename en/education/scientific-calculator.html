What Is a Scientific Calculator

A scientific calculator is an advanced calculator designed to perform complex mathematical calculations. Unlike a basic calculator that handles only addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division, a scientific calculator supports functions such as powers, square roots, trigonometric functions, logarithms, and calculations using mathematical constants like π and e.

The CalcCore online scientific calculator works directly in your browser and can be used for mathematics, engineering, physics, education, and everyday calculations that require more advanced math operations.

Functions Available in the Calculator

This scientific calculator supports a wide range of mathematical operations, including:

  • basic arithmetic: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division
  • powers and exponents: x², xʸ
  • square roots: √x
  • trigonometric functions: sin, cos, tan
  • logarithms: log and ln
  • absolute value: |x|
  • reciprocal function: 1/x
  • parentheses for complex expressions
  • mathematical constants: π and e
  • angle modes: DEG (degrees) and RAD (radians)
  • calculation history and the ability to reuse the previous result using Ans

How to Use the Scientific Calculator

Using the calculator is simple. Enter your mathematical expression using the buttons or your keyboard, and then press = to get the result. For complex expressions you can use parentheses to control the order of operations.

Examples:

  • (2 + 3) × 4 = 20
  • sqrt(144) = 12
  • 2^3 = 8
  • sin(90) in DEG mode = 1
  • sin(π/2) in RAD mode = 1

DEG vs RAD Mode

Trigonometric functions depend on the angle measurement mode used by the calculator.

  • DEG — angles are measured in degrees
  • RAD — angles are measured in radians

For example, sin(90) equals 1 when the calculator is set to DEG mode, while sin(π/2) equals 1 in RAD mode. If a trigonometric result seems incorrect, check which mode is currently active.

Order of Operations

The calculator follows the standard mathematical order of operations:

  • operations inside parentheses
  • powers and roots
  • multiplication and division
  • addition and subtraction

For example, the expression 2 + 3 × 4 equals 14, not 20, because multiplication is performed before addition. Using parentheses changes the order: (2 + 3) × 4 = 20.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a scientific calculator used for?

A scientific calculator is used for advanced mathematical calculations such as trigonometric functions, logarithms, powers, square roots, and complex expressions. It is commonly used in mathematics, engineering, physics, and technical fields.

What is the difference between a basic calculator and a scientific calculator?

A basic calculator performs simple operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. A scientific calculator supports more advanced functions such as trigonometry, logarithms, exponents, and calculations with mathematical constants like π and e.

What do DEG and RAD mean?

DEG stands for degrees and RAD stands for radians. These are two different units used to measure angles in trigonometric functions. If you calculate sin(90) in DEG mode you get 1, while sin(π/2) equals 1 in RAD mode.
