Volume Calculator — How it works

This calculator helps you quickly find the volume of geometric solids. You can calculate the volume of a cube, sphere, or cylinder.

Volume of a cube

If the edge length of the cube is known, use the formula:

  • V = a³

Here a is the edge length of the cube.

Volume of a sphere

For a sphere, the formula is:

  • V = 4/3 × π × r³

Here r is the radius of the sphere.

Volume of a cylinder

For a cylinder, use the formula:

  • V = π × r² × h

Here r is the radius of the base and h is the height of the cylinder.

Example calculation

If a cylinder has radius 3 and height 10, then:

  • V = π × 3² × 10 = π × 9 × 10 = 90π ≈ 282.7433

What is useful about this calculator

The calculator automatically shows the formula, intermediate steps, shape visualization, and the final result. Both whole and decimal values are supported.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you find the volume of a cube?

Raise the edge length to the third power: V = a³.

How do you find the volume of a sphere?

Use the formula V = 4/3 × π × r³.

How do you find the volume of a cylinder?

Multiply the area of the circular base by the height: V = π × r² × h.

Which shapes does the calculator support?

A cube, a sphere, and a cylinder.

Can I enter decimal numbers?

Yes, both whole and decimal values are supported.

What value of π is used?

The standard mathematical value of π is used with sufficient precision for practical calculations.
